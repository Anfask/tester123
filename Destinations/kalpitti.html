Kalpitti Island

Kalpitti Island, also spelled Kalpatti, is a small, uninhabited island that is part of the Agatti Atoll in Lakshadweep, India. It's located at the southern tip of Agatti Island, another island in the Lakshadweep archipelago known for its pristine beaches and laid-back atmosphere.

Kalpitti Island itself is known for its natural beauty and is a popular destination for tourists who visit Agatti Island. The island is home to a nesting ground for sea turtles, and there were plans to extend the runway of the Agatti Airport onto Kalpatti Island to accommodate larger aircraft. However, these plans were thankfully rejected due to environmental concerns.

Since Kalpitti Island is uninhabited, there are no facilities available for tourists on the island itself. However, visitors can enjoy swimming, snorkeling, and sunbathing on the island's beaches. The island is also a great spot for birdwatching, as it is home to a variety of bird species. If you're looking for a place to relax and enjoy the beauty of nature, Kalpitti Island is a great option. Just be sure to plan your trip accordingly, as there are no amenities available on the island.

Kalpitti Island Facts

Location : It is located 8 km from Agatti.

Latitude : 10.8134° North

Longitude : 72.1674° East

Climate : Tropical. 22 to 30 c.

Languages : Malayalam

How to get there : Air India operates flight from Kochi to Agatti every day and then by boat to Kalpitti Island.

Food & Cuisine : Spiced coconut-rich Vegetarian and Non vegetarian food.

Tourist Season : October to March is deemed the best time to visit Lakshadweep, as the sea is calmer and the weather is pleasant during this time. During the summer season, i.e. March to May; the temperature varies between 22C and 36C. This is the ideal time to indulge in water sports and adventure activities.

Things not to do : Consumption of alcoholic beverages is prohibited in all islands. Picking up Corals is a punishable crime.
